Fit check

Need to compare the formats more closely?

These notes are secondary context for fit, format, and implementation. The main service paths stay visible above.

AI Permit to AI License The flagship path from AI literacy to responsible classroom workflows.

The full series gives school teams shared language, practical routines, and evidence habits for using AI without losing sight of student thinking.

  • Starts with AI literacy, expectations, and responsible-use language.
  • Builds toward teacher and student workflows with verification and authorship built in.
  • Can run as a multi-session series, district PD arc, or 90-day implementation pilot.
Workshops & District Sessions Focused PD formats for staff days, departments, leadership teams, or county groups.

These sessions work when a team needs a clear entry point before committing to the full pathway or when a single audience has a specific AI learning need.

  • 60-90 minute sessions, half days, full days, or multi-session workshops.
  • Common focus: responsible AI use, verification, prompting, policy language, and classroom workflow design.
  • Can lead into AI Permit to AI License when the team is ready for a deeper arc.
Speaking Clear, grounded talks for conferences, school events, and leadership groups.

Speaking works best when the audience needs a clean frame for AI without a tool-demo parade or hype-heavy pitch.

  • Keynotes, student-facing assemblies, conference sessions, or staff kickoff events.
  • Can be framed around AI literacy, responsible use, or the production framework.
AI Permit Game A hands-on classroom or library game for responsible AI literacy.

Best fit for librarians, media specialists, media literacy classes, technology teachers, digital citizenship programs, and schools that want a concrete way to introduce responsible AI use.

  • Introduces verification, authorship, evidence, and judgment through play.
  • Can support the broader framework while still working as a standalone classroom activity.
Coaching & Custom Support Follow-through for teams working through a specific lesson, rollout, or workflow problem.

If the need is specific, the work can be scoped around the actual problem: a department, a leadership question, a classroom workflow, or a new initiative.

  • Useful for leadership teams, departments, pilots, or one-on-one planning.
  • Starts with context first, then a recommendation on the lightest useful format.
