AI Permit -> AI License Series

A clear path from understanding AI to intentional use.

Educators move through a structured pathway that builds literacy, practical application, responsible use, and stronger workflows for teaching and learning.

Pathway overview

What the series builds

Available as a two-day series, full-day workshops, or individual sessions

Foundational understanding

  • AI literacy and understanding
  • Practical classroom applications

The series starts by helping educators understand what AI is, how it works, and where it fits into real practice.

Intentional implementation

  • Responsible AI use
  • Structured workflows for teaching and learning

Each part of the Permit -> License pathway can also be delivered as a focused presentation or workshop.

Individual workshops

Each part of the pathway can also be booked on its own.

Workshops typically run 60-90 minutes and can be combined.

What Is AI

Clear definitions, common misconceptions, and practical framing for educators and teams.

How AI Works

Models, training data, tokens, and probability explained in accessible language.

AI History

The context behind today's tools, from earlier systems to the current wave of generative AI.

AI Applications

Examples of how AI can support planning, feedback, communication, and workflow efficiency.

Ethical AI

Bias, transparency, authorship, and responsible use framed for real-world decision-making.

Prompting

Better inputs, better outputs, and a clearer process for getting useful results from AI tools.

Data & Privacy

What staff need to know about information sharing, student data, and responsible tool use.

Verifying AI

How to check outputs, spot weak responses, and build habits that prevent overreliance.

The Modes of AI

Understand how different AI roles support different tasks so use stays intentional.

Designing AI-Supported Workflows

Build practical systems for teaching, learning, or team operations with clear checks and next steps.
